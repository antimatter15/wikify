More

Project Wikify is not just a bookmarklet. It's a web based platform for online communication over static information. The Bookmarklet just happens to be the (as of now) primary use. A Read-Only Bookmarklet, Userscript, and a super-crappy standalone web browser, exists as well.

Userscript

The Userscript is a way to automatically read "wikifed" content on the internet, without constantly having to click the "Wikify" button. Userscripts work natively (albeit somewhat complicated) on Opera, easily on Firefox with the Greasemonkey extension, and on Safari and IE with other extensions.

On Firefox, just go to this script after Greasemonkey is installed and enabled, and for every other web browser, do a google search (I'm sooo helpful :-)).

Read-Only Bookmarklet

If you are not among the supported browsers, you'll probably have a better shot with the Read-Only bookmarklet. It is much faster than the normal one, more seamless, and less buggy. It doesn't let you edit, but sometimes you don't want an interface that somewhat interfears with the website's navigation (though Project Wikify has a Toggle button that disables editing, and does the more-or-less equivalent of this bookmarklet).

To install, follow the same procedures as the Wikify Bookmarklet, but with this Read-Only Wikify link instead.
